**1. The `--vad-silence-db` parameter is entirely dead code in the vocal detection pipeline.**
You are correct. In `detect_vocal_windows`, the `silence_db` argument is accepted in the signature but never evaluated. Silero VAD does not operate on RMS dBFS levels; its underlying neural network outputs a framewise posterior probability of speech presence based on learned spectral features. The script relies entirely on `get_speech_timestamps` applying a `0.30` probability threshold to these scores. The `-40.0` dB intent from the CLI is silently ignored during vocal detection, creating a false impression of user control. (Note: The drum detection stage _does_ correctly utilize its own `drum_silence_db` parameter via `_compute_rms_db`).

**2. Distributional shift on separated stems requires recalibration, and 0.30 is highly risky.**
Silero VAD expects a natural noise floor and mixed acoustic environments. When fed a Demucs vocal stem, the statistical texture changes drastically. The absence of background noise typically pushes the model's speech-probability scores higher (often pegging near 1.0 during speech). However, the primary risk on separated audio is **separator artifacts**—specifically, transient bleed from snares or synths that Demucs mistakenly leaves in the vocal stem.

Because Silero was not trained to classify "Demucs bleed" as non-speech, these artifacts frequently trigger false positives. By lowering the onset threshold to `0.30` (from the default `0.50`), the script becomes _more_ permissive. While this ensures faint melismatic tails are captured, it significantly increases the vulnerability to separator bleed triggering false vocal windows. The threshold must be empirically calibrated against the specific artifact profile of `htdemucs`, and 0.30 is likely too low for reliable rejection of bleed.

**3. The 200 ms internal minimum does not discard speech; it pre-merges silences.**
There is no risk of the 200 ms parameter pre-discarding short vocal events. In the `get_speech_timestamps` wrapper, `min_silence_duration_ms` dictates the minimum duration a probability score must remain _below_ the threshold to trigger a segment split.

If a breath gap or micro-pause is shorter than 200 ms, the wrapper simply ignores the dip and keeps the speech segment open. If the gap is longer than 200 ms, the wrapper splits the segment. Because the script's secondary application-level loop subsequently merges any segments separated by less than 6 seconds (`silence_duration`), the 200 ms internal bridge is entirely subsumed by the 6-second bridge. The two stages do not conflict; the first stage simply performs a micro-merge before the second stage performs a macro-merge.
